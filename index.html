Leadership Team and Advisors

Andrew Teravskis and Adam Gregory are the co-CEOs of Noble. Shortly after launching, they secured development capital and acquired rights to several world-class stories including the Cedric King story (ENDURANCE) and the Andy Andrews novel THE HEART MENDER. In 2022, they extended their development financing capabilities by partnering with executive producer Raymond Harris. In 2025, they launched Noble Creator Network, a platform for investing in and supporting YouTube creators.

Our executive team is supported by an advisory board. Board chair Mark Holman is the Chairman of global pharmaceutical company Expanesthetics and former owner of an independent production company. Dana Wichterman is a founding members of Wedgwood Circle, and is active in influencing culture through public policy and the arts. Raymond Harris is an executive producer, founder of one of the largest architectural firms in the US, and serves on the Board of Directors for the Come and See Foundation, the non-profit sponsor of THE CHOSEN.

HUGE IMPACT, SMALL SCREENS

A YouTube competition reality show in the vein of Beast Games and Survivor. Students from all over the United States can apply to compete in a series of qualifying games, before the top 12 head to the finals… to compete for a $100,000 scholarship.

Mission: To create heroes out of college students worth emulating.

We’ve produced a pilot episode (linked above), and have secured $550,000 of the $750,000 production budget for Season 1.

View Channel

A YouTube show hosted by New York Times bestselling author and historian Joseph Loconte. The show consists of fast-paced video essays tracing the history of Western Civilization… often through an unexpected lens.

Mission: To irrigate cultural and spiritual deserts with a reminder of the classical Christian traditions of the West.

We’ve produced two episodes in the GIFT OF THE ITALIANS mini-series (Episode 2 linked above), and four episodes in the BENJAMIN RUSH mini-series (releasing this month on YouTube). All production has been financed largely by brand sponsors. To date, the channel has received upwards of $150,000 in brand sponsor deals and $60,000 in grant funds.
